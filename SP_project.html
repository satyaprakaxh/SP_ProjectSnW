Sir Jagadish Chandra Bose

Bose was the first person in the world to demonstrate the wireless transmission of signals using millimeter-wave radio waves (in 1895, before Marconi's famous demonstration). He invented an early form of a semiconductor detector (the "coherer") which was a crucial component of early radio

Thomas Edison

Thomas Edison's main inventions include the phonograph (recording sound), the first practical incandescent light bulb, and the motion picture camera. He also created the first electric power distribution system to light up cities, and held over 1,000 patents for inventions like the alkaline battery.

Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla invented the Alternating Current (AC) electrical system, the polyphase AC motor, and the Tesla Coil. His work is the foundation of the modern power grid that delivers electricity to our homes and factories. He also pioneered radio technology, remote control, and wireless energy transfer.

Dr. Narinder Singh Kapany

The Genius Behind The Internet- Dr. Narinder Singh Kapany, the "Father of Fiber Optics," was the first to successfully demonstrate transmitting images through flexible glass fiber bundles. He coined the term "fiber optics," and his pioneering invention laid the foundation for modern medical endoscopes and the high-speed fiber optic cables that power the internet.

Timeline of Major Inventions

  • 1450s: Johannes Gutenberg invents the printing press.
  • 1769: James Watt improves the steam engine, sparking the Industrial Revolution.
  • 1876: Alexander Graham Bell invents the telephone.
  • 1903: Wright Brothers achieve first powered flight.
  • 1990s: Tim Berners-Lee invents the World Wide Web.
